Preface¶

This is the final article in a series about Glanceable Displays and my efforts to create one for my household. For other articles in the series, click on the title of the article under the heading “Glanceable Displays” on the right side of the webpage.

Introduction¶

In the previous article Glanceable Displays: Setting Up Our Display, I added the finishing touches to allow a Raspberry Pi to display a single web page as a Glanceable Display. It took a bit of time to get there, but the work that I put in over the previous articles made sure that the foundation for the Glanceable Display is solid, a necessity for a device that I want to be as “hands-off” as I can be.

What Are My Options?¶

As of the writing of this article, there are 3 options available:

  • roll your own
  • use Dakboard
  • use Magic Mirror

Option 1: Roll Your Own¶

Pros Cons
write it yourself write it yourself

Writing down a list of pros and cons for this option, I had the same thing on both sides: write it yourself. On the pros side, you have the freedom to do what you want, however you want to do it. On the cons side, you have the responsibility to learn and create what you want, as well as maintaining it, debugging it, and hosting it. While this is the option with the greatest ability to be creative, it is also the option with the greatest costs.

Option 2: Use Dakboard¶

Pros Cons
professional solution locked in to their pricing scheme
they do the dev and testing
multiple screens available
loops of screens available

This option uses the frequently sited DAKboard site to host and maintain the webpages to display.

Option 3: Use Magic Mirror¶

Pros Cons
lots of third party plugins oriented to sharing a single screen

What Did I Decide?¶

For my purposes, Dakboard’s solution was the best fit for what I wanted to do.

What Was Accomplished¶

This article detailed the work that was needed to take my Raspberry Pi from a sufficiently set up machine to a glanceable display. After noting some simple requirements for my glanceable display, I proceeded to add some tools to the machine that were needed for the configuration changes that followed. In case someone is following what I am doing for their own glanceable display, I detailed some of the debugging steps that I used to check to make sure that my configuration changes were working.

With this accomplished, I was confident that whatever I decided to display on my glanceable display would be on device that was solidly set up.

What’s Next?¶

In the final article in this series, Glanceable Displays: What Do I Want To Display? I walk through the steps I took to determine where to get finalized webpages to display in my glanceable display.
